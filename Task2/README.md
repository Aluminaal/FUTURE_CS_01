# 🔐 Task 2: Phishing Detection and Awareness

## 📌 Overview
This task focuses on identifying phishing attacks and understanding how attackers use social engineering techniques to manipulate users. The objective is not only to detect phishing attempts but also to raise awareness about how to prevent them.

---

## 🎯 Objective
- To analyze phishing email samples  
- To identify common phishing indicators  
- To classify the level of risk associated with such attacks  
- To understand how phishing attacks work  
- To promote cybersecurity awareness and safe practices  

---

## 🧪 Methodology
The following steps were followed during the analysis:

1. Collected phishing email examples from publicly available educational resources  
2. Examined email components including sender, subject, body, and links  
3. Identified suspicious patterns and phishing indicators  
4. Analyzed attacker techniques used to deceive users  
5. Classified the risk level based on the findings  
6. Documented prevention strategies and awareness guidelines  

---

## 📧 Phishing Email Scenario
The analyzed phishing emails attempt to create urgency by informing the user that their account is at risk (e.g., account deletion or password expiration). The user is instructed to click a link and take immediate action.

These emails are designed to appear legitimate but contain hidden malicious intent.

---

## 🚩 Phishing Indicators Identified

- ⚠️ Urgency and Fear Tactics  
  Messages such as “Your account will be deleted within 24 hours” are used to pressure users.

- 🔗 Suspicious Links  
  Links redirect to unknown or fake websites instead of official domains.

- 👤 Fake Sender Identity  
  Email addresses do not match the claimed organization.

- 📩 Generic Greetings  
  Use of terms like “Dear User” instead of the recipient’s actual name.

- 🚨 Call-to-Action Buttons  
  Buttons like “Cancel Request Immediately” are used to trick users into clicking.

---

## ⚠️ Risk Classification
Risk Level: High

Phishing attacks can lead to:
- Credential theft  
- Unauthorized account access  
- Data breaches  
- Financial loss  

---

## ⚙️ How the Attack Works
1. The attacker sends a phishing email disguised as a trusted source  
2. The email creates urgency or fear  
3. The victim clicks a malicious link  
4. A fake website is displayed  
5. The victim enters sensitive information  
6. The attacker captures and exploits the data  

---

## 🛡️ Prevention Tips
- Always verify the sender’s email address  
- Avoid clicking on unknown or suspicious links  
- Check URLs carefully before entering credentials  
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)  
- Use updated antivirus and security tools  

---

## ✅ Do’s and ❌ Don’ts

### ✔ Do’s
- Verify the authenticity of emails  
- Hover over links before clicking  
- Report suspicious emails  

### ❌ Don’ts
- Do not share personal or login information via email  
- Do not trust urgent messages blindly  
- Do not click unknown attachments or links  

---

## 🧠 Awareness and Impact
Phishing attacks target human behavior rather than system vulnerabilities, making them highly effective. Increasing user awareness is one of the most important defenses against such attacks.

Organizations should educate users and implement security measures to reduce risks.
