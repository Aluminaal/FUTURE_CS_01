# Phishing Detection & Awareness Report

## 1. Introduction
Phishing is one of the most common cyber attacks used to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, personal data, and financial details. Attackers use fake emails that appear legitimate to deceive users.



## 2. Objective
The objective of this report is to:
- Analyze phishing email samples
- Identify phishing indicators
- Classify email risk levels
- Provide awareness and prevention guidelines



## 3. Methodology
The analysis was conducted by manually reviewing email content. The following elements were checked:
- Sender email address
- Message tone (urgency or fear)
- Links and URLs
- Requests for sensitive information



## 4. Email Analysis

### 🔴 Email 1 – Phishing

- **Classification:** Phishing  
- **Email Scenario:**  
  An email claiming account suspension and asking the user to verify immediately using a link.

- **Phishing Indicators:**
  - Fake or suspicious sender domain
  - Urgent and threatening language
  - Suspicious link URL
  - Generic greeting

- **Impact:**  
  Users may enter credentials on fake websites, leading to account compromise.

- **Recommendation:**
  - Do not click unknown links
  - Verify official website manually
  - Check sender carefully



### 🟡 Email 2 – Suspicious

- **Classification:** Suspicious  
- **Email Scenario:**  
  A job offer email from an unknown sender requesting personal information.

- **Indicators:**
  - Unknown sender
  - No company identity
  - Request for personal data

- **Impact:**  
  Possible data misuse or social engineering attack.

- **Recommendation:**
  - Do not share personal information
  - Verify sender identity
  - Research the organization



### 🟢 Email 3 – Safe

- **Classification:** Safe  
- **Email Scenario:**  
  A legitimate account notification email from a trusted service.

- **Indicators:**
  - Trusted sender
  - No suspicious request
  - Informational content

- **Impact:**  
  No immediate risk.

- **Recommendation:**
  - Always verify notifications
  - Enable account security features



## 5. Common Phishing Indicators
- Fake email addresses
- Urgent or threatening messages
- Suspicious links
- Requests for sensitive data
- Generic greetings



## 6. Prevention & Awareness Guidelines

### Do’s:
- Verify sender email addresses
- Check links before clicking
- Use official websites
- Enable two-factor authentication

### Don’ts:
- Do not click unknown links
- Do not share passwords or OTPs
- Do not trust urgent messages blindly



## 7. Conclusion
Phishing attacks rely on human error rather than system vulnerabilities. By understanding phishing indicators and following basic security practices, users can effectively protect themselves from such attacks.
